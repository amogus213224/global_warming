    Global warming

WHAT IS GLOBAL WARMING?

Average climate indicators are constantly changing. It is believed that the Earth has already warmed by 1.2 degrees due to industrial emissions, also called greenhouse gases. The countdown starts from 1880, that is, before the industrial revolution. And although it seems that 1.2 degrees is not much, scientists said four years ago, that an increase in temperature of 1.1 degrees due to additional heat and energy equal to four Hiroshima bomb explosions per second. Since the 1980s, each decade has been warmer than the previous one. In general, scientists say that an increase in the planet’s temperature to 2 degrees will lead to disaster: There will be problems with agriculture, a number of animals will die, in general, warming will affect all ecosystems. Weather anomalies will worsen in different parts of the planet, which will also affect people's lives. Scientists expect less rain in some parts of the world and in another there will be more of them, in the third part there will be the same amount of precipitation, but it will be stronger and shorter-lived. Such anomalies will cause drought and floods.

WHAT ARE THE CAUSES OF GLOBAL WARMING?

Greenhouse gases existed before industry. These are volcanic eruptions, forest fires and even breathing of living organisms, that is, this is a normal process. On the other hand, now the impact of industry so great that the process went tens of times faster. Reasons for the increase in emissions: Combustion of coal, oil and gas Deforestation Increase in livestock production Nitrous oxide emissions from fertilizers Emission of fluorinated gases from equipment and products According to Carbon Majors, 100 companies were responsible for 71% of emissions from 1988 to 2015, The Guardian reports.

WHAT SHOULD WE DO ABOUT THIS?

Different experts offer different options for greening: switching from cars to bicycles, stop using disposable tableware, eating meat and giving birth. The last one is no joke. Scientists really believe that reducing childbearing can be one of the effective measures. For example, a study at Oregon State University found that every child born in the USA will add 9.4 tons to harmful emissions into the atmosphere. However, without children on the planet in at some point it will become empty, so UN environmental experts suggest continuing to give birth, But: fly less reduce waste, isolate your home, travel on foot or by bicycle, there are seasonal products, turn off electrical appliances, wear clothes until the last moment, talk about greening with friends and at work, switch to renewable energy sources, use cashless payment, eat more plant foods, repair things and give them a “second life”. The general tendency of all these tips is to use as much energy and resources as possible, and if everyone does this, then we can make a tangible contribution to the ecology of the earth.
